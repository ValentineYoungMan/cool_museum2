ABOUT THE EXHIBITION
Meet the Great Masters
A special set of guided tours of a Bernini and Caravaggio exhibition let you dive in the most valuable masterpieces brought together in The Metropolitan Museum of Art.

TICKETS
How to attend the exhibition?
The admission to the exhibition is only via pre-booked tickets with a chosen time slot.
Hurry up! The exhibition changes over time.
Carefully study this website and select the tour that suits you best by the date and time. Please note that the exhibition will last only 4 days, and the number of tickets is limited.
We also want to draw your attention to the fact that tickets are sold on pre-order only through this official site. If you see tickets for the Bernini and Caravaggio exhibition somewhere else on sale, please let us know and in no case buy tickets there. Most likely, they are fake, and you will not be able to use them to enter the museum.
On this website, you can purchase tickets to such guided tours:
  • Grand opening
  • Adult group
  • Family tour
  • Closure event
Pre-book tickets
Once you have chosen the date and time of the guided tour, fill out the form at the top of this website. You need to specify your name, e-mail, and phone so that our managers can get in touch with you.
In a couple of hours, you will get an e-mail or a call from our manager who will tell you about all the pricing options and the availability of the tickets. Remember that the quantity of tickets to each guided tour is limited, so don't hesitate to make an order.
If you confirm your order, you'll receive an invoice with the number of tickets and their overall price. You need to pay for your order within 2 working days.
Pay the Admission Fee
Remember that your ticket for the guided tour doesn't give you access to the whole MET museum. To get to the museum and go to a temporary exhibition, you will also need to pay the admission fee. We have three options for such tickets. If you already have a ticket for regular admission to the museum, you do not need to buy an additional one - in this case, you pay only for the guided tour.
A basic ticket allows you to attend all the permanent exhibitions in the Metropolitan Museum of Art. A VIP ticket gives you access to permanent exhibitions and all the lectures held on the day of your visit. Annual tickets provide you access to all exhibitions, lectures and special events within a year, you can visit the museum as many times as you want.
Wait for the Confirmation
After you pay for the tickets, we need some time to process the payment and confirm the purchase. We expect a great demand for these tickets, so it is possible that during the processing of the transaction tickets run out.
In this case, you will receive an additional letter or a call from our manager with a proposal to change the ticket to another date or time. If you want to refuse the purchase at this stage, you can do this by calling us.
If your transaction is confirmed, you will receive tickets on your email within 2 days after the payment. Each ticket has its own QR-code and a confirmation number.
Be sure to print your ticket or download the MET app.
Enjoy the exhibition
We recommend that you come in advance, as in MET there are always lines at the entrance to the museum itself. We have some rules and restrictions:
  • No smoking in the museum rooms (including electronic cigarettes);
  • No pets allowed, with the exception of guide dogs for visually or partially seeing visitors;
  • All visitors pass a security check;
  • It is forbidden to bring alcohol and food to the territory of museums (they can be left in the cloakroom, but if not picked up at the end of the day, they will be recycled);
  • It is forbidden to touch the exhibit items;
The use of drones, including photo and video shooting is prohibited.

FAQ
Frequently asked questions
Please read this section carefully, it reflects the most common problems which our visitors face. If you still have questions, you can ask us directly.
Information about us
Where is the central entrance of the museum?
The central entrance is located right in the center of the building, you can access it from the E 82nd Street.
Is there a cloakroom in the museum?
There are two cloakrooms in the building, on the right and left sides in the main lobby. In order not to lose time, be sure to check where there is less queue.
What are the working hours?
Our working hours are: Monday - Thursday and Sunday: 10:00 - 17:30; Friday - Saturday: 10:00 - 21:00 Please note that the museum’s standard opening hours do not apply to temporary exhibitions and special events.
Are there any restrictions for visits?
Before entering the museums, visitors should leave backpacks, suitcases, bags, umbrellas, tripods and other oversized luggage in excess of the permissible size (40 * 35 * 15 cm) in the cloak room. It is forbidden to carry knives, scissors, any kind of firearms. Laser pointers are prohibited.
Information about guided tours
Are there any tours for kids?
You can join our family tour with our guide Lola Pitts. It's a specially designed tour for families with children, and the program is made up to be not boring for small kids.
Can I make photos or videos?
Each of the masterpieces has a special sign that shows if it's allowed. Some of the works are presented without safety glass - in this case, it's not allowed to make photos of them with flash.
Can I buy the tickets directly at the museum?
Unfortunately, the guided tours on the Bernini & Caravaggio exhibition need pre-booking in advance, as only small groups of people are allowed to visit within one time slot. You can book your tickets on this site.
Can I return the purchased tickets?
No, you can't return the purchased tickets, but you can hand or sell them to somebody, as they are not individual and don't contain your name.
